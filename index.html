About me

I've always been driven by the desire to blend creativity, convenience, beauty, and meaningful impact in my work—to create solutions that truly make life better. Growing up as an avid gamer, I loved exploring the creative mods people made for my favorite games. These mods not only enhanced the gaming experience but also sparked my curiosity. I would download the mod files and peek into the code, marveling at how simple lines of text could transform a game’s visuals or mechanics. There were some mod files where we could comment in what features we wanted to apply. I didn’t understand much of it back then, but I really wished I could. I wanted to create mods just as beautiful, creative, and impactful as the ones I’d seen and used.

That early fascination drove me to experiment. The first script I ever wrote was a simple continuous clicker in Python. It was a small project, but seeing it work made me want to learn more. As I grew older, my interests shifted toward a career in healthcare, and I pursued a degree in biochemistry with the goal of becoming a pharmacist. I believed that by being a healthcare worker and providing essential medications, I could help people lead healthier, fuller lives—just as software engineers use their skills to solve real-world problems and improve daily life.

During my biochemistry studies, I spent countless hours in the lab, using their softwares that were confusing to use and honestly, not that great. Then as I was gaining experience as a pharmacy technician, I noticed that many their softwares were also clunky, inefficient, and not user-friendly. This frustration brought back memories of my early modding days, when I enjoyed mods that significantly improved the UI and UX of my favorite games and when I had the same struggles trying to understand what code. That spark reignited my passion for technology. I realized the healthcare system—vital as it is—deserves better technology.

Determined to bridge this gap, I started taking online courses, beginning with Python. Many of the classes were focused on building simple games. I had so much fun seeing my creations come to life with just a few lines of code, and I was reminded of that initial excitement from my youth modding games.

Today, as a full-stack software engineer with a foundation in healthcare and science and an ever-growing love for games, I bring a unique perspective to the table. My experiences have highlighted numerous opportunities to refine software and UI/UX design for greater efficiency and effectiveness. While I have skills in JavaScript, Python, and full-stack development, I’m adaptable and eager to learn new languages and technologies to achieve my goals. I’m driven to create innovative solutions that enhance system functionality, user experience, and the immersive quality of gaming environments.

Outside of work, I’m a houseplant enthusiast—I have a whole collection of plants that I love taking care of. I also love cats and often find myself working on cat-themed projects for fun. Whether I’m figuring out how to make healthcare software more efficient or dreaming up cool ideas for games, I’m all about blending creativity, tech, aesthetics, and practicality. With my background in healthcare, technical skills, and love for games, I’m excited to build software that makes a real difference and brings a little more fun and ease into everyday life. I can’t wait to team up with others to create innovative solutions that have a real impact—just like those early game mods made on me!
